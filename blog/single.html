Inclusive Community at VCU

Chuck Klink • Student Focused • Oct. 16, 2016

As we draw to the end of the spring semester, I want to acknowledge that for many students this is a time of stress and increased external pressure. Although these feelings are normal, they can be difficult to manage at times. Even if this is not your experience, you may be aware of those around you who are experiencing these feelings as the semester draws to a close.

If you are feeling stressed, I encourage you to reach out to others â€“ family, friends, advisors, faculty members, and supportive others. It is helpful to reflect back on other stressful times to remind yourself that you navigated your way through the difficulties. Practice good self-care, focus on nutrition, schedule study breaks, get enough sleep, exercise and avoid alcohol and other drugs. Utilize available resources including VCU Counseling Services, www.students.vcu.edu/counseling/, to talk with someone at the counseling center or utilize their self-help resources. Visit the Wellness Resource Center, www.thewell.vcu.edu/, to discuss stress management techniques or to utilize the many resources on their website.

Sometimes when an individual is in distress and feeling overwhelmed they may think about suicide. We need to let members of our community know that suicide is not a solution to psychological pain that is temporary. There is treatment for depression and other issues that lead to suicidal thinking that is highly effective. If you or someone you know needs assistance, please use the available resources below:

  • University Counseling Services: (804) 828-6200 (Monroe Park Campus); 828-3964 (MCV Campus)
  • There is an after hours on-call counselor available by calling (804) 828-1234 and asking to talk with the counselor
  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 1-800-784-2433

I would encourage each one of us to be mindful of ways that we can encourage and support one another. Let someone know that you care about them and support them. We each have a responsibility to let individuals know that help is all around them and that seeking help is the right step during difficult times. Share with a fellow student or faculty member (the end of the semester is also stressful for them!) gratitude for how they have positively contributed to your life.

I wish you the very best as you finish the semester and appreciate your efforts to make VCU a truly caring community.
